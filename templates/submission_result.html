Description of Variants

Baseline: This is a room with a window facing in the specified direction. No shading is applied and there is no ventilation (i.e. the windows are closed all year). The construction is lightweight (wood), i.e. there is little thermal mass.

+ Shading: The same as the Baseline, but a dynamic shading system is applied to the window that closes once the air temperature in the room exceeds 20°C.

+ Vent. Day: The same as “+ Shading”, but the windows of the room open between 7 am and 6 pm, whenever the air temperature of the room exceeds 22°C and the relative humidity of the outdoor air is under 70%.

+ Vent. Night: The same as “+ Vent. Day”, but the windows of the room always, whenever the air temperature of the room exceeds 22°C.

+ Thermal Mass: The same as “+ Vent. Night”, but the construction in heavier (e.g. concrete or rammed earth), i.e. there is thermal mass.

PMV Metric

PMV, or Predicted Mean Vote, is a widely used index in the field of Building Science to quantitatively measure human thermal comfort. Developed by P.O. Fanger in the 1960s, PMV is based on the heat balance of the human body. It predicts the mean value of the votes of a large group of people on a seven-point thermal sensation scale, ranging from -3 (too cold) to +3 (too hot).
